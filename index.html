Software Engineering Training

While studying at Watch And Code, I embraced the process of learning software engineering primarily by reading open source codebases. The course is designed to guide students from complete beginner to contributing in a meaningful way to open source projects. After finishing the core curriculum, I spent about six months as an active contributor to the devtools/debugger of Mozilla Firefox Web Browser. It was a great experience to have my code reviewed by Mozilla engineers, and to digest and contribute to such a massive codebase. Tackling this modern Javascript codebase I became famaliar with React and Redux, learned to use Mercurial for version control, and wrote tests using Jest and Mochitest frameworks.

I am curently studying as a Software Engineer Fellow at Qwasar Silicon Valley where I have learned numerous languages and frameworks, with a focus on the fundamentals of software engineering, including data structures and algorithms. By building many small projects which piggyback each other, I have gained an increased understanding of larger patterns in software architecture. There is an emphasis on peer code reviews (both giving and recieving), working both individually and as part of a small team, and helping others by making presentations about specific concepts or previously completed projects.

Biography

For fifteen years I enjoyed a successful career as an orchestral musician. I held positions playing French Horn with many orchestras around the country, including the Rochester and Louisiana Philharmonic Orchestras, Sarasota Opera Orchestra, and San Francisco Chamber Orchestra. I had the privelege to perform and tour frequently with the San Francisco Symphony, and participated in many recording sessions, solo, chamber music, and festival performances. I also held teaching positions at Centre College and San Jose State University.

In 2017 I was diagnosed with Task Specific Focal Dystonia, which effectively ended my performing career. I worked for one year as a professional brewer, then made the decision to immerse myself in the study of software engineering. I currently work as an Admin Manager for Awesome Pawsome, where I enjoy spending time with clients (human and canine) and helping manage the complexities of running a thriving small business in the heart of San Francisco. I am passionate about learning and pursuing a lifelong continuous growth mindset, and I'm eager to begin working professionally as a developer.

When I'm not immersed in learning to code, I enjoy reading, movies, brewing beer, and distance running.
